# CoreThink 12-Month Projection Plan

**Prepared by:** Dylan  
**Purpose:** Provide a concise projection plan covering pricing, customer acquisition channels, customer growth, and the investment required to execute.  
**Scope:** First 12 months for a founding DevRel / Growth Engineering / Partnerships owner.

---

## 1. Executive Summary

The recommended 12-month plan converts the CoreThink/OpenClaw hybrid strategy into a measurable go-to-market motion:

> **Local-first adoption -> paid design-partner pilots -> production annual contracts.**

CoreThink should position the motion around a simple commercial thesis: builders are adopting AI agents quickly, but they still need better economics, stronger security posture, and more reliable reasoning on hard planning, coding, and cybersecurity workflows. The plan uses technical proof, ecosystem integrations, design-partner pilots, and targeted enterprise outreach to turn that thesis into pipeline and bookings.

| Question | Recommendation |
|---|---|
| **Pricing** | Use a three-layer ladder: free/community adoption, **$20K-$30K design-partner pilots**, and **$75K-$150K production annual contracts plus usage**. |
| **Acquisition channels** | Prioritize technical documentation, ecosystem integrations, Bay Area field motion, web3/security account-based outreach, and enterprise reasoning/planning outreach. |
| **Customer growth** | Base case: **400 activated builders/champions**, **35 qualified opportunities**, **6 paid pilots**, **4 production annual contracts**, **10 cumulative paying logos**, and **$510K in year-one bookings**. |
| **Execution investment** | Fund a **$200K founding DevRel / Growth Engineering / Partnerships role** plus a **$50K operating budget** for tooling, content, demos, integrations, events, and partner campaigns. |

The salary and operating budget are execution costs, not customer pricing. Customer pricing should remain separate and be tested through pilots and annual contracts.

## 2. Positioning

CoreThink is strongest when framed as **safe hybrid AI for agentic builders**:

1. **Local default:** small and mid-sized local models handle frequent routine work.
2. **CoreThink escalation:** CoreThink is used for planning, multi-file reasoning, security-sensitive judgment, root-cause analysis, and synthesis.
3. **Local control:** files, repo state, shell policy, memory, and durable execution remain in the user's workspace.

The market message should stay connected to CoreThink's four core pillars: **domain-specific languages, improved coding, improved cybersecurity, and improved planning**.

The wedge narrative:

> **AI agents are still expensive, inconsistent, and difficult to trust on high-stakes work. CoreThink provides a safer hybrid path: keep routine work local, escalate only high-value reasoning, and preserve local control.**

This should be proven through documentation, demos, integrations, security posture, and repeatable evidence rather than broad awareness marketing.

## 4. Pricing Model

The pricing ladder should mirror familiar developer-tool and AI-tool buying motions: free adoption, paid pilots, and annual production contracts.

| Commercial layer | Recommended year-one price | Customer value | Funnel role |
|---|---:|---|---|
| **Community entry** | Free | Docs, repos, OpenClaw/CoreThink configs, safe-hybrid quickstarts, benchmark cookbooks, integration notes. | Trust and adoption |
| **Design-partner pilot** | **$20K-$30K for 6-8 weeks** | Scoped implementation against one real workflow, including local routing, CoreThink escalation, evals, security rules, and an outcome report. | Early revenue and proof |
| **Production annual contract** | **$75K-$150K annual base plus usage** | Ongoing hybrid routing, evaluation support, integration maintenance, field support, and roadmap feedback loop. | Repeatable revenue |
| **Strategic enterprise** | **$150K-$300K+ custom** | Larger deployments with security review, governance, compliance, private environment needs, SSO, or deeper field-engineering support. | Upside cases |

**Base-case assumptions:** average pilot of **$25K**, average first-year annual contract of **$90K**, year-one bookings target of **$510K**, and break-even threshold of **$250K** total investment.
