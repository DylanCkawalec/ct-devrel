## 2. Strategy that matches the original CoreThink/OpenClaw pitch

The original strategy is strongest when positioned as **safe hybrid AI for agentic builders**:

1. **Local default:** small/mid local models handle high-volume routine work.
2. **CoreThink escalation:** CoreThink handles planning, multi-file reasoning, security-sensitive judgment, root-cause analysis, and synthesis.
3. **Local execution and memory:** files, repo state, shell policy, memory, and durable execution stay in the user's workspace.

The commercial message should stay tied to CoreThink's four original pillars:

- Domain-specific languages
- Improved coding
- Improved cybersecurity
- Improved planning

The wedge narrative is:

> **Your agents are costing too much and still failing on hard tasks. CoreThink gives you a safe hybrid path: keep routine work local, escalate only high-value reasoning, and preserve local control.**

This matters because the market is not asking for another generic AI assistant. Developers are adopting AI tools, but they are still skeptical of accuracy and security. That means the acquisition motion should not be broad hype marketing; it should be **docs, demos, integrations, security posture, and repeatable proof**.

## 4. Pricing model to test

The recommended pricing ladder mirrors what buyers already understand in developer-tool and AI-tool markets: free/open-source entry, usage or seat-based team adoption, and custom enterprise contracts.

| Commercial layer | Year-one recommended price | What the customer gets | Role in funnel |
|---|---:|---|---|
| **Community entry** | Free | Docs, repos, OpenClaw/CoreThink configs, safe-hybrid quickstarts, benchmark cookbooks, integration notes. | Trust and adoption |
| **Design-partner pilot** | **$20K-$30K for 6-8 weeks** | A scoped implementation against one real workflow: local routing, CoreThink escalation, evals, security rules, and an outcome report. | Early revenue and proof |
| **Production annual contract** | **$75K-$150K annual base plus usage** | Ongoing hybrid routing, evaluation support, integration maintenance, field support, and roadmap feedback loop. | Repeatable revenue |
| **Strategic enterprise** | **$150K-$300K+ custom** | Larger deployment with security review, governance, compliance, private environment needs, SSO, or deeper field-engineering support. | Upside cases only |

### Forecast assumptions

For the base case below, use:

- **Average paid pilot:** $25K
- **Average first-year annual contract:** $90K
- **Year-one bookings target:** $510K
- **Break-even investment threshold:** $250K total investment, consisting of $200K salary plus $50K operating budget

These are planning assumptions, not observed CoreThink performance.

- **Total base-case year-one bookings = $510K**

This is bookings, not recognized GAAP revenue.

### Projected mix by segment

| Segment | Activated builders | Paid pilots | Annual contracts | Expected bookings contribution |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|

### Break-even logic

The requested year-one execution investment is **$250K**. The base case crosses that threshold during Q3. A downside break-even path is:

- 2 annual contracts plus 3 paid pilots, or
- 3 annual contracts with limited pilot revenue.
